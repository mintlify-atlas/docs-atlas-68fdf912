title: "Monitor tire status and punctures in AutoZone ITLA"
description: "See the condition of every tire by axle and position, update status as tires wear, and log puncture incidents with a description and date."
---

The Tires section (Estado de Gomas) gives you a live view of every tire on your vehicle. Each tire is identified by its axle (`eje`) and position (`posicion`) on that axle, making it easy to track wear and incidents wheel by wheel. The number of tires shown matches the `cantidadRuedas` you set when you registered the vehicle.

## How tire positions work

Each tire record has two positioning fields:

- **`eje`** — the axle the tire belongs to (e.g. "delantero" for front axle, "trasero" for rear axle)
- **`posicion`** — the side on that axle (e.g. "izquierdo", "derecho")

Together, `eje` and `posicion` uniquely identify a wheel. The list screen shows this combination in the subtitle of each tile, for example: "Eje: delantero • Estado: BUENA".

## Tire status values

Each tire carries one of four status values:

| Status | Meaning | Color indicator |
|---|---|---|
| `buena` | Tire is in good condition | Green |
| `regular` | Tire shows some wear; monitor closely | Orange |
| `mala` | Tire is in poor condition; replace soon | Red |
| `reemplazada` | Tire has been replaced | Blue |

The colored dot on the left side of each tire card reflects the current status at a glance.

## Updating a tire's status

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the tire card">
    On the Estado de Gomas screen, tap a tire card to expand it. The card shows the current `posicion`, `eje`, and `estado`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Estado">
    Tap the **Estado** button inside the expanded card. A dialog opens listing all four status options: BUENA, REGULAR, MALA, and REEMPLAZADA.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the new status">
    Tap the status that reflects the tire's current condition. The dialog closes, the update is sent to the server immediately, and the card's color indicator refreshes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Status changes take effect immediately and are not reversible through the app. If you select the wrong status, simply open the card again and set the correct one.
</Warning>

## Logging a puncture

You can record a puncture (pinchazo) on any tire without changing its status. Punctures are stored as a history log attached to the specific tire.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Expand the tire card">
    Tap the card for the affected tire to expand it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Pinchazo">
    Tap the **Pinchazo** button (orange). A dialog opens with two fields: **Descripción** and **Fecha**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Describe the incident">
    Enter a short description in **Descripción** — for example, "Clavo en la vía principal" or "Pinchazo por objeto en autopista".
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm or change the date">
    The **Fecha** field pre-fills with today's date. Tap it to open a date picker if the puncture happened on a different day.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the puncture">
    Tap **Registrar**. The puncture is saved and appears in the incident history at the bottom of the expanded tile.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Viewing puncture history

When a tire has one or more recorded punctures, the expanded card shows a **Historial de Pinchazos** section below the action buttons. Each entry lists the `descripcion` and `fecha` of the incident.

<Tip>
  After replacing a tire, update its status to `reemplazada` and log a final puncture entry if the replacement was triggered by a flat. This keeps your history accurate if you ever sell the vehicle or review records later.
</Tip>
