The Mi Perfil screen gives you a snapshot of your account information and lets you update your profile photo without leaving the app. You need to be logged in to access it — profile data is fetched from the server using your session token.

<Note>
  Mi Perfil requires an active login session. If you are not signed in, the app redirects you to the login screen first.
</Note>

## What your profile shows

When the screen loads, the app calls `GET /perfil` and displays the following fields from the `UserProfile` model:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **foto** (`fotoUrl`) | Your profile photo, shown as a circular avatar at the top |
| **nombre** | Your first name |
| **apellido** | Your last name |
| **correo** | Your email address, shown below your name |
| **matricula** | Your ITLA student ID number |
| **rol** | Your assigned role in the system |
| **grupo** | Your assigned group |

Your full name (`nombre` + `apellido`) and `correo` appear in the header banner. Your `matricula`, `grupo`, and `rol` are listed as info tiles below.

## Read-only fields

All profile fields are read-only — the screen does not include a form for editing your name, email, or other text data. The only thing you can change directly from this screen is your profile photo.

## Updating your profile photo

Tap your photo (or the camera icon that overlays it) to replace it. The app opens your device gallery via `image_picker`, lets you choose an image, and uploads it automatically.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap your profile photo">
    On the Mi Perfil screen, tap the circular avatar at the top. A camera icon in the bottom-right corner of the avatar indicates it is tappable.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a photo from your gallery">
    Your device gallery opens. Choose the image you want to use as your profile photo.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for the upload to complete">
    The app sends the image to `POST /perfil/foto` as a `multipart/form-data` request with a `foto` field. Once the upload succeeds, the screen refreshes and your new photo appears immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The upload request uses this structure:

```http
POST /perfil/foto
Content-Type: multipart/form-data

foto: <image file>
```

On success, the server returns a `fotoUrl` with the URL of your new photo.

<Note>
  The server automatically compresses uploaded images to a maximum of 100 KB. You can upload images up to 20 MB — no manual resizing needed.
</Note>

## Other options on this screen

The Mi Perfil screen also gives you access to app-level settings:

- **Modo Oscuro** — toggle between light and dark theme
- **Idioma** — switch the app language between Español and English
- **Acerca De** — navigate to the About screen with development team info
- **Logout** (top-right icon) — end your session and return to the login screen
